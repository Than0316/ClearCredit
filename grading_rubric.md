# ML1 Final Project — Grading Rubric v2 (2025–2026)

## Overview

The final project is assessed through **two equally important deliverables**:

1. **Python Notebook** (50 points total)
2. **Oral Presentation** (50 points total)

**Total: 100 points**

The notebook is evaluated as a **combined technical artifact and written report**. It must both:
- function as a correct, reproducible Python notebook, and
- communicate the student's analysis, modeling choices, results, and conclusions clearly.

The oral presentation is evaluated separately and accounts for **half of the final project grade**. Students are therefore expected not only to produce a strong notebook, but also to present and defend their work clearly and convincingly.

Track-specific challenges remain important, but in this version they are assessed **within the notebook and presentation criteria**, rather than as a separate grading block.

---

## 1. Python Notebook (50 points)

The notebook serves as both:
- the **code submission**, and
- the **project report**.

It should be readable from top to bottom by an instructor who wants to understand the problem, the data, the methods, the results, and the reasoning behind the decisions.

### A. Code Functionality, Organization, and Reproducibility (15 points)

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 15 | All code runs correctly from start to finish. Notebook is cleanly organized into logical sections. Variable names are clear, repeated logic is factored when appropriate, and results are reproducible (for example via fixed random seeds). |
| 12 | Code runs with only minor issues or warnings. Structure is mostly clear, with limited inefficiencies or organizational problems. Reproducibility is mostly maintained. |
| 9 | Code runs in most places but includes some bugs, fragile cells, or inconsistent structure. Reproducibility is partial or not clearly ensured. |
| 6 | Notebook contains several execution or organization issues that make it difficult to follow or rerun. |
| 0 | Code does not run reliably or contains major logical errors. |

### B. Data Analysis and Exploratory Work (10 points)

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 10 | Data loading, inspection, and exploratory analysis are thorough. Relevant statistics and visualizations are included. Important patterns, anomalies, imbalance, skewness, outliers, or correlations are identified and interpreted. |
| 8 | EDA is solid and includes most important checks and plots, with generally good interpretation. |
| 6 | Basic EDA is present but lacks depth, breadth, or interpretation. |
| 4 | EDA is superficial and misses key diagnostics or insights. |
| 0 | Minimal or incorrect exploratory analysis. |

### C. Modeling, Evaluation, and Experimental Rigor (15 points)

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 15 | A clear baseline is established. Multiple reasonable models are tested. Model comparison is rigorous, with appropriate validation and metrics. Hyperparameter tuning, residual analysis, error analysis, or confusion-matrix analysis are used where relevant. Choices are justified. |
| 12 | Modeling is competent and compares more than one approach, with mostly appropriate validation and evaluation. |
| 9 | Modeling is acceptable but limited in diversity, rigor, or evaluation depth. |
| 6 | Modeling is weak, poorly justified, or insufficiently evaluated. |
| 0 | No meaningful modeling workflow is demonstrated. |

### D. Narrative Quality and Reporting Clarity (10 points)

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 10 | The notebook reads like a clear technical report. Markdown sections, explanations, comments, and conclusions guide the reader through the full workflow. Decisions are motivated, limitations are discussed, and conclusions are tied back to the project question. |
| 8 | The notebook is well explained overall, with only minor gaps in narrative clarity or justification. |
| 6 | Some explanation is present, but the reader must infer important reasoning or transitions. |
| 4 | The notebook has limited narrative structure and does not clearly communicate the reasoning behind the work. |
| 0 | The notebook is essentially code-only or lacks intelligible explanation. |

## 2. Oral Presentation (50 points)

The oral presentation is a major component of the project grade. It should clearly communicate the problem, the analysis, the modeling decisions, the key results, and the lessons learned.

### A. Structure, Clarity, and Time Management (15 points)

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 15 | Presentation is very clearly structured, easy to follow, and well paced. It includes a clear introduction, a logical progression through the project, and a strong conclusion. Time is managed effectively. |
| 12 | Structure is clear overall, with only minor pacing or transition issues. |
| 9 | Presentation is understandable but has uneven structure or pacing. |
| 6 | Presentation is difficult to follow because of weak organization or poor time management. |
| 0 | Presentation is incoherent or not meaningfully delivered. |

### B. Technical Understanding and Depth (15 points)

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 15 | The speaker demonstrates strong understanding of the dataset, the challenges, the modeling choices, and the results. Key trade-offs, limitations, and alternative approaches are discussed intelligently. |
| 12 | Technical understanding is solid, with appropriate explanations of the main choices and findings. |
| 9 | The main workflow is described correctly, but explanations remain somewhat superficial. |
| 6 | Technical discussion is weak, incomplete, or largely descriptive without real understanding. |
| 0 | The presentation does not demonstrate meaningful technical understanding. |

### C. Communication Quality and Use of Evidence (10 points)

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 10 | Speech is clear and confident. Visuals are well chosen and directly support the argument. Results are explained accurately and convincingly. |
| 8 | Communication is clear overall and visuals are used effectively, with minor weaknesses. |
| 6 | Communication is adequate but sometimes unclear, rushed, or weakly supported by visuals. |
| 4 | Communication is difficult to follow or visuals are poorly integrated. |
| 0 | Communication is ineffective or presentation support is absent. |

### D. Defense, Q&A, and Critical Reflection (10 points)

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 10 | The speaker answers questions clearly and accurately, reflects critically on limitations, and can justify methodological decisions under discussion. |
| 8 | Questions are answered competently, with only minor uncertainty. |
| 6 | Answers show partial understanding but lack confidence or precision. |
| 4 | Responses are vague, incomplete, or weakly justified. |
| 0 | The speaker cannot answer basic questions about the project. |
